## Purpose

This plan describes a FIA-based workflow for testing whether post-disturbance
recruitment shifts toward species associated with warmer or drier realized
climate niches.

The central question is:

> Do naturally disturbed FIA forest conditions recruit species with warmer or
> drier climate niches than comparable undisturbed control conditions?

The current implementation builds the analysis inputs. It does not yet contain
final matched-control models or inferential results. Reportable findings should
only be added after they are generated by committed scripts and accompanied by
QA outputs.

## Analysis Scope

The current response variable is tree seedling recruitment, measured at the FIA
condition-visit level:

```text
stable_plot_id x PLT_CN x INVYR x CONDID
```

Seedlings are used as the first recruitment layer because FIA records seedlings
as species-level regeneration data. Sapling, tree, shrub, forb, and graminoid
products are being prepared so later analyses can extend beyond tree seedlings.

## Core Inputs

### FIA Community And Disturbance Products

- `plot_seedling_species.parquet`: species-level seedling abundance by condition
  and subplot, aggregated to condition level before CWM calculation.
- `plot_condition_metadata.parquet`: stable plot identity, condition identity,
  forest type, coordinates, and forest status.
- `plot_disturbance_classification.parquet`: natural-disturbance class,
  treatment/harvest flags, control eligibility, and disturbance timing fields.

FIA records conditions within plot visits. A single plot visit can contain more
than one condition, and those conditions can differ in forest type, disturbance,
and treatment history. The current workflow therefore evaluates recruitment and
disturbance eligibility at the condition level rather than collapsing immediately
to the whole plot.

### Species Climate Niches

Species climate niches are produced in `06_species_niches/` from BIEN range maps
overlaid with TerraClimate 1981-2010 monthly climate. The compact niche product
contains eight indicators:

- Annual mean temperature
- Warmest-month temperature
- Coldest-month temperature
- Temperature seasonality
- Annual climate water deficit
- Peak monthly climate water deficit
- Annual precipitation
- Driest-month precipitation

The preferred thermophilization input uses study-area niches with a documented
global fallback for species whose FIA observations occur in the study area but
whose BIEN range polygons do not overlap the study-area climate extraction.

## Planned Analytical Design

### 1. Define Eligible Disturbed And Control Conditions

The analysis cohort already identifies:

- `disturbed_vs_control`
- `disturbance_class`
- `disturbance_class_primary`
- `is_control_candidate`
- `is_natural_disturbance_candidate`
- `has_cutting_treatment`
- `has_human_dist_condition`

Natural-disturbance classes are based on FIA condition disturbance codes. Harvest
and human-disturbance flags are retained for exclusion or sensitivity analysis.

### 2. Retain Niche-Coverage Diagnostics

The CWM product retains rows with incomplete niche coverage and reports:

- `frac_weight_with_niche`
- `frac_weight_with_study_area_niche`
- `frac_weight_with_global_fallback_niche`
- `meets_niche_coverage_threshold`

The primary analysis should either filter to conditions meeting the selected
coverage threshold or include coverage as a documented sensitivity criterion.

### 3. Match Disturbed Conditions To Controls

The planned matched-control design should compare disturbed conditions to
control conditions with similar:

- Forest type (`FORTYPCD` or `forest_type_group`)
- Region or ecoregion
- Inventory period
- Baseline climate
- Recruitment niche coverage

The matching script should write an analysis-ready table with one row per
disturbed-control pair and explicit match diagnostics.

### 4. Estimate Disturbance Effects

The primary effect metric should be:

```text
delta_CWM = disturbed condition CWM - matched control CWM
```

Primary responses:

- `cwm_temp`
- `cwm_cwd`
- `cwm_pr`
- Additional compact indicators where scientifically justified

Positive `delta_cwm_temp` indicates recruitment weighted toward warmer-niche
species relative to controls. Positive `delta_cwm_cwd` indicates recruitment
weighted toward species associated with higher climate water deficit.

### 5. Run Sensitivity Checks

Recommended sensitivity checks:

- Require `frac_weight_with_niche >= 0.95`.
- Compare study-area-only niches to study-area niches with global fallback.
- Compare seedling weighting by `seedlings_tpa`, raw count, and presence/absence.
- Compare `FORTYPCD` to broader forest-type grouping.
- Compare East/West grouping to ecoregion or climate-region grouping.
- Evaluate whether plot-level exclusion warnings materially affect results.

## Data Issues To Carry Forward

The following items are documented QA considerations, not reasons to stop the
workflow:

- Some FIA or P2VEG source codes are pseudo taxa, genus-level records, unknowns,
  or infraspecific taxa. These are excluded or flagged in the species-niche
  workflow.
- Some accepted names do not have BIEN range maps. These are documented in
  species-niche QA outputs.
- A small number of FIA-observed species require global fallback niches because
  the BIEN range does not overlap the configured study-area extraction.
- Some conditions have incomplete CWM coverage because one or more recruiting
  seedling species lacks a usable niche.

The authoritative status files are listed in:

- [Species Niches QA Guide](../06_species_niches/qa/README.md)
- [Thermophilization README](../07_thermophilization/README.md)

## Reporting Standards

Final results should be added only when:

1. The result is generated by a committed script.
2. The input and output paths are documented.
3. QA files report sample sizes and coverage.
4. The sign convention is explicit:

   ```text
   disturbed - matched control
   ```

5. Limitations are stated next to the result, not separated into informal notes.

Until those conditions are met, this document should remain a plan and workflow
overview rather than a findings memo.
