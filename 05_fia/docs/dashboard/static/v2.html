02 · Spatial Tour

Big picture → small picture

The FIA inventory nests measurements inside one another. Each scale collects different things — tap a step to zoom in, see what's measured there, and follow a real plot all the way down.

Six scales · click to focus

Climate, terrain, biome — the geographic context for everything below.
Step 02 · National grid
FIA plot network
A systematic hexagonal sample — one plot per ~6,000 acres.
Step 03 · Plot
PLOT — 4-subplot cluster
Four subplots arranged on a 120-ft layout. The single point identified by PLT_CN.
Step 04 · Subplot
Subplot — 24-ft radius
Where trees DBH ≥ 5″ are measured. Each subplot may overlap one or more conditions.
Step 05 · Microplot
Microplot — 6.8-ft radius
Inside each subplot — saplings (1″–4.9″ DBH) and seedlings counted here.
Step 06 · Tree
Individual tree
Species, DBH, status, damage agent, mortality — one row per stem.

The key insight: every FIA table joins back to PLT_CN. But within a plot, different tables work at different scales — TREE is at the tree level, COND is at the condition (subplot region) level, and PLOT itself is at the cluster level. Picking the right grain is half the battle.

How to join FIA tables: nearly every question starts at PLOT and joins outward. Add COND for forest-type, ownership and disturbance; TREE for tree-level metrics; SEEDLING for regeneration. Always carry (PLT_CN, INVYR) as your join key — it identifies a unique plot visit.

The FIADB schema

How the tables link

Everything joins to PLOT.CN. Every non-reference table has a PLT_CN column that equals PLOT.CN. Within a plot, finer grains add keys: CONDID for conditions, SUBP for subplots, TREE for trees. Reference tables (Ch 11, prefixed REF_) join to their parent through code columns like SPCD, FORTYPCD, DAMAGE_AGENT_CD, etc.
Source of truth: all definitions on this page come from the FIADB User Guide — Volume: Database Description, version 9.4 (USFS, August 2025). When the user guide and a query result disagree, trust the user guide.

Chapter 2: Database Tables — Location Level. The geographic and administrative backbone of FIADB. PLOT is the central record; everything else joins back via PLT_CN.

SectionTableWhat it holds

COND.DSTRBCD1/2/3 and COND.DSTRBYR1/2/3 — up to three disturbance codes (with years) per condition. Grain: PLT_CN × INVYR × CONDID. Codes from FIADB User Guide section 2.5.37 (rev 08.2025).

Reporting threshold: a disturbance is only recorded if it affected at least 1 acre and caused mortality or damage to ≥ 25% of the trees in the condition. Events smaller than that don't make it into the database. The window is since the last measurement (typically 5–10 yr) for re-measured plots, or within the last 5 years for new plots.

DAMAGE_AGENT_CD vs AGENTCD vs DSTRBCD: DAMAGE_AGENT_CD (this table, FHAAST 5-digit) is the granular per-tree damage list. AGENTCD is a 2-digit summary used as the cause-of-death on dead trees. DSTRBCD is the condition-level disturbance code (see Disturbance tab). The three answer different questions and don't always agree — pick the one matching the grain you need. Appendix H has ~1,000 specific agent codes; the FIADB SQLite is the source of truth.

COND.TRTCD1/2/3 — up to three silvicultural treatment codes per condition, same 1-acre / 25% threshold as disturbances. Window: since last measurement (5–10 yr) for re-measures, or last 5 years for new plots (some FIA work units extend to 20 years where PLOT.MANUAL < 1.0). Codes from FIADB User Guide section 2.5.43 (rev 08.2025).

TRTCDTreatmentDefinition
00No observable treatment—
10 Cutting harvest The removal of one or more trees from a stand.
20 Site preparation site prep Clearing, slash burning, chopping, disking, bedding, or other practices clearly intended to prepare a site for either natural or artificial regeneration.
30 Artificial regeneration planted Following a disturbance or treatment (usually cutting), a new stand where at least 50% of the live trees present resulted from planting or direct seeding.
40 Natural regeneration natural Following a disturbance or treatment (usually cutting), a new stand where at least 50% of the live trees present (of any size) were established through the growth of existing trees and/or natural seeding or sprouting.
50 Other silvicultural other Fertilizers, herbicides, girdling, pruning, or other activities (not covered by 10–40) intended to improve the commercial value of the residual stand; or chaining on woodlands to encourage wildlife forage.
Treatment vs disturbance: if it was a deliberate silvicultural action by humans it goes in TRTCD; if it was unintentional (insect outbreak, fire, wind, beaver flood) it goes in DSTRBCD. Both have three ordered slots per condition. To exclude recently harvested plots from an analysis, filter COND.TRTCD1/2/3 for code 10 (Cutting) and the matching TRTYR within your window.

Chapter 3: Database Tables — Tree Level (10 tables) and Chapter 6: NRS Tree Regeneration (3 tables, regional). One row per stem, with the GRM family supporting growth, removal & mortality estimation between visits.

SectionTableWhat it holds
3.1TREEOne row per measured tree (DBH ≥ 5″) per visit. The main tree-level table — species, diameter, height, status, damage, biomass, carbon, volume, position. Joins to PLT_CN, CONDID, SUBP.
3.2TREE_WOODLAND_STEMSFor multi-stemmed woodland species (e.g. juniper), records each stem's diameter and length when the species is measured at the root collar (d.r.c.).
3.3TREE_GRM_COMPONENTGrowth/Removal/Mortality component assignments — classifies each tree as survivor, ingrowth, harvest, mortality, etc. used for net-change estimates.
3.4TREE_GRM_THRESHOLDTree attributes at the moment the tree crossed a measurement threshold (e.g. grew into the 5″ DBH minimum).
3.5TREE_GRM_MIDPTTree attributes at the midpoint between two visits, used for annualized growth estimation.
3.6TREE_GRM_BEGINTree attributes at the beginning of the GRM interval (typically the previous visit values).
3.7TREE_GRM_ESTNPer-tree GRM estimation results — final per-acre contributions to growth, removal, mortality totals.
3.8BEGINENDCounter table indicating how many times to access a tree record in TREE_GRM_ESTN when computing net-growth accounting estimates.
3.9SEEDLINGOne row per species per microplot per visit — tally of seedlings (DBH < 1″ and ≥ 6″ tall) by species. TREECOUNT is the per-microplot count.
3.10SITETREESite index trees — selected dominant/codominant trees used to estimate site productivity. Drives SITECLCD.
6.1PLOT_REGENNRS-specific plot-level regeneration summary (sapling and seedling counts, browse damage).
6.2SUBPLOT_REGENNRS-specific per-subplot regeneration tally.
6.3SEEDLING_REGENNRS-specific per-species seedling regeneration counts with more detail than SEEDLING.
The GRM family: the five TREE_GRM_* tables together support FIA's growth, removal & mortality accounting. You usually don't query them directly — you join through the population (Ch 9) tables, which weight per-tree records into population-level totals.

Chapter 4: Database Tables — Invasive Species; Understory Vegetation. Three tables capturing percent-cover data for invasive plants and Phase 2 understory vegetation. All grain at the subplot level, joining to SUBP_COND for the parent condition.

SectionTableWhat it holds
4.1INVASIVE_SUBPLOT_SPPPercent cover of invasive species identified on the subplot. Species codes resolve via REF_PLANT_DICTIONARY.SYMBOL (the NRCS PLANTS database).
4.2P2VEG_SUBPLOT_SPPPhase 2 vegetation: percent cover by individual species identified on the subplot. Same plant dictionary linkage as 4.1.
4.3P2VEG_SUBP_STRUCTUREPhase 2 vegetation: percent cover by layer (e.g. tree, shrub, forb, graminoid) and growth habit — structural summary rather than species-by-species.
Joining vegetation to a plot: (PLT_CN, CONDID, SUBP) links each veg record to its parent SUBP_COND; species codes (VEG_SPCD) link to REF_PLANT_DICTIONARY.SYMBOL; invasive-only species lists live in REF_INVASIVE_SPECIES (section 11.8).

Chapter 9: Population (7 tables) and Chapter 10: Plot Geometry / Snapshot (2 tables). These tables make FIADB a statistical inventory rather than a raw plot listing — they define evaluations, strata, and expansion factors that turn individual plots into population-level estimates.

SectionTableWhat it holds
9.1POP_ESTN_UNITEstimation units — geographic strata used in expansion (typically county or county group).
9.2POP_EVALNamed evaluations (e.g. “CA2019”) that bind a set of plots, an estimation method, and a time window together for analysis.
9.3POP_EVAL_ATTRIBUTEAttribute-level metadata for each evaluation (which attributes were estimated, calibration info).
9.4POP_EVAL_GRPEvaluation groups bundling related evaluations (e.g. all attribute types for a state-year).
9.5POP_EVAL_TYPEvaluation type lookup (volume, growth, mortality, area, change, etc.).
9.6POP_PLOT_STRATUM_ASSGNAssigns each plot to a stratum within an estimation unit for a given evaluation.

10.1PLOTGEOMPlot geometry — precise (un-fuzzed) plot location attributes used internally. Public copies may be restricted.
10.2PLOTSNAPSnapshot of plot-level attributes at a specific evaluation, frozen for stable point-in-time estimates.
Do not sum plot records directly to get state or regional totals. Each plot record represents many acres of the population — the expansion is held in POP_STRATUM.EXPNS. Pick a POP_EVAL.EVAL_GRP matching your question and join through POP_PLOT_STRATUM_ASSGN.

05 · Plot Design

The 4-subplot national field protocol

Every FIA Phase 2 plot uses the same fixed-radius, fixed-azimuth design so that measurements are directly comparable across the country. One central subplot, three satellites, four nested microplots, and (when needed) a larger macroplot for big trees.

Standard layout

Why this exact shape?

FIA wants a single sample design that works in dense Pacific Northwest forest, sparse southwestern woodland, and cleared agricultural land. The four-subplot cluster strikes a balance: it covers enough ground to capture within-stand variability without being so spread out that crews can't measure it all in a day.

The fixed 120-ft layout means analysts can pool data across the country knowing every plot was measured the same way. Trees are tallied once on the subplot they fall on; saplings and seedlings go on the smaller microplot because they are too dense to count efficiently across a full subplot.

Total footprint: ~ 1 acre. Field crew time: roughly half a day per visit for a typical forested plot.

Each microplot center is offset 12 ft due east (azimuth 90°) from its subplot center. Tree positions within a subplot are recorded as AZIMUTH (degrees from north) and DIST (horizontal distance, ft).

The three FIA phases

Phase 1

remote

Photo and satellite interpretation across a fine-resolution sample grid. Used to stratify the landscape by forest/non-forest and improve the precision of Phase 2 estimates.

Phase 2

field

The 4-subplot field plots described on this page. ~1 plot per 6,000 acres, ~140K plots nationwide. The bulk of FIA's tree-level data lives here.

Phase 3

subset

~1/16 of Phase 2 plots, with extra ecological measurements: soils, lichens, downed wood, vegetation profiles, ozone bioindicators, crown condition. ~6,000 plots in the rotation.

Annual panel system (since 1999)

Each state's plots are split into panels (typically 5 in the East, 10 in the West). One panel is measured each year, so a given plot is re-visited every 5–10 years on a rotating cycle. This gives FIA both a current snapshot (the 5- or 10-year moving window) and direct re-measurement for growth, mortality, and removal estimates.

Pre-1999 data is different: historical (“periodic”) inventories re-measured an entire state in one campaign every 7–15 years. The plot designs varied between regions and over time — the DESIGNCD field tells you which protocol was in use. Modern annualized plots all use DESIGNCD = 1.
Plot design codes (FIADB Appendix G, rev 06.2021)

Almost every modern plot uses DESIGNCD = 1. The 100+ historical and regional variants documented in Appendix G are grouped here by FIA Work Unit. They mostly differ in how trees were tallied (fixed-radius vs variable-radius prism points), microplot sizes, and whether conditions were mapped or rotated.
